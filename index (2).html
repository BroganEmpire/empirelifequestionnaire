Sum assured required
If you are unsure of any amounts, leave blank — your adviser will discuss these with you. Enter figures as numbers only, without commas (e.g. 250000).

A lump sum paid to your family or dependants if you die during the policy term. Commonly used to pay off a mortgage or replace lost income. For example, if your mortgage is £200,000 you may want at least that amount of cover.

A lump sum paid to you if you are diagnosed with a serious illness listed in the policy (such as cancer, heart attack or stroke). This money is yours to use however you need — paying off a mortgage, adapting your home, or covering everyday costs while you recover.

A monthly income paid to you if you are unable to work due to illness or injury. This replaces a proportion of your salary — typically up to 60–70% of your gross income — and continues until you return to work, retire, or the policy ends. Enter the monthly amount you would need to cover your essential outgoings.

How long you want the cover to last. Many people match this to the length of their mortgage (e.g. 25 years), or choose cover until a specific age such as retirement. Your adviser can help you decide the right term for your circumstances.

Private medical insurance

Private medical insurance (PMI) gives you fast access to private healthcare when you need it most, bypassing NHS waiting lists. It typically covers the cost of private consultations, specialist referrals, diagnostic tests, surgery and hospital stays for acute (short-term) conditions. PMI can be taken out for yourself, your partner, or your whole family, and premiums vary based on your age, health history and the level of cover you choose. Many people find it gives them peace of mind knowing that if something goes wrong, they can be seen and treated quickly.
